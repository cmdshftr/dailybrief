Daily Stoic — 15 March // The Ides of March
"Even if you're going to live three thousand more years, or ten times that, remember: you cannot lose another life than the one you're living now, or live another one than the one you're losing. The longest amounts to the same as the shortest."
— Marcus Aurelius, Meditations, 2.14
Today is the Ides of March — the day Julius Caesar was assassinated in 44 BC, a reminder that no position, no power, and no plan is guaranteed beyond this moment. Marcus Aurelius, who inherited an empire shaped by that very event, wrote constantly about the brevity of life. The Stoic lesson: treat today's decisions as if they are the only ones that matter — because they are.
